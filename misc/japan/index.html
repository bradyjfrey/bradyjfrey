Kyoto

京都

Anime, Gaming & Kimono

  1. Pokémon Center Kyoto: SUINA Muromachi; Heian-period Pikachu plushies in kimono, exclusive to this location.
    0
  2. Kimono Rental Wargo Gion: Large selection, English-friendly, around 3,300 yen.
    0
  3. Kimono Rental Okamoto: Five locations near Kiyomizu and Gion; over 1,000 kimonos in stock at all times.
    0
  4. Tōei Kyoto Studio Park: Uzumasa; samurai-drama backlot you can walk through, ninja shows.
    0
  5. Kyoto International Manga Museum
    0

Food, Markets & Atmosphere

  1. Nishiki Market: Kyoto's Kitchen; 400-meter covered market.
    0
  2. Sanjō-kai Shotengai: Local covered shopping arcade; less touristed than Nishiki, where locals actually shop.
    0
  3. Pontocho Alley: Lantern-lit dining alley along the Kamogawa.
    0
  4. Sannen-zaka & Ninen-zaka: Preserved sloped streets en route to Kiyomizu; full of food, sweets, and crafts.
    0
  5. Demachi Futaba: Legendary mame mochi shop, Imadegawa.
    0
  6. Arabica Higashiyama: The original location of the global coffee export.
    0
  7. Wife & Husband Cafe: Kitaoji, near the Kamo River; reservation-required coffee shop that also rents picnic baskets with thermos, cups, tablecloth, and folding chairs for a riverbank picnic. 90-minute rental around ¥500.
    0

Temples, Gardens & History

  1. Kinkaku-ji: The Golden Pavilion.
    0
  2. Ginkaku-ji: The Silver Pavilion; start of the Philosopher's Path.
    0
  3. Ryōan-ji: The iconic Zen rock garden.
    0
  4. Fushimi Inari Taisha: The red torii tunnel; go early or at dusk.
    0
  5. Kiyomizu-dera: World Heritage Site; the wooden stage with city views.
    0
  6. Arashiyama: Bamboo Grove, Tenryū-ji, and Iwatayama Monkey Park.
    0
  7. Ōkōchi Sansō Villa & Garden: Arashiyama; the former mountain villa of silent-film actor Denjirō Ōkōchi, up past the bamboo grove. Paid entry but significantly less crowded; admission includes matcha and a sweet.
    0
  8. Sanjūsangen-dō: 1,001 standing Kannon statues.
    0
  9. Nijō Castle: Tokugawa shogun's Kyoto residence; nightingale floors.
    0
  10. Kyoto Imperial Palace & Kyoto Gyoen National Garden: Free; the palace proper is a guided tour, surrounding gardens are open for walking.
    0
  11. Tō-ji: Five-story pagoda, walking distance from Kyoto Station.
    0
  12. Daitoku-ji: Sub-temple complex, less crowded, Zen gardens.
    0
  13. Kyoto National Museum
    0
  14. Philosopher's Path: Canal-side walk between Ginkaku-ji and Nanzen-ji; cherry trees.
    0
  15. Murin-an Garden: Higashiyama/Okazaki; Meiji-era stroll garden designed by Ogawa Jihei VII, uses the Higashiyama mountains as borrowed scenery. Historically significant as the site of the 1903 pre-Russo-Japanese War council.
    0
  16. Kyoto Railway Museum: 53 trains including steam locomotives; great model dioramas.
    0
  17. Kamo River Northern Walk: Free; walking north from the Gion and Pontocho area along the Kamogawa gets progressively quieter and more local.
    0

    0
